Comic

"And it's only by linking positive things that the negative can be delinked. The 99 references the 99 attributes of Allah in the Koran, things like generosity and mercy and foresight and wisdom and dozens of others that no two people in the world would disagree about."

-- Dr. Naif Al-Mutawa

The 99 is a comic based on the 99 attributes of Allah. Each of the 99 characters are teenagers name for one of these attribute; for example, Aleem -- the all-knowing, Batina -- the hidden and Samda -- the invulnerable. The characters hail from across the globe and unite in their pursuit of social justice and peace against the forces of chaos and evil. Since the comic involves teenagers around the world, it allows school age children from all backgrounds to find a common ground.

About the Author

Dr. Naif A. Al-Mutawa, the creator of The 99, hoped to create a positive outlook for the Muslim community by creating a pop culture that would reflects the good intentions of Islam. Dr. Naif as a young lad loved comics himself. He first discovered comics during his stay at a Jewish summer camp. Dr. Naif earned his PhD in Clinical Psychology from Long Island University.
